Learn About Cat Breeds

What are breeds?

Cat breeds are distinct populations of cats that have been selectively bred by humans to consistently exhibit specific physical and behavioral traits. These traits are passed down genetically from parent to offspring, maintaining the distinctive characteristics of each breed.

Modern cat breeds are developed through careful selection of cats with desired traits and controlled breeding over many generations. This process has resulted in over 70 recognized breeds worldwide, each with their own genetic profile, appearance standards, and temperament tendencies.

While all domestic cats belong to the same species (Felis catus), breeds represent genetic subpopulations with distinctive, predictable characteristics that set them apart from random-bred cats.

How do these breeds affect my cat's health?

Breeds can significantly impact a cat's health due to the genetic traits that have been selected and concentrated within each breed population. While careful breeding practices aim to produce healthy cats, the genetic bottlenecks created during breed development can sometimes lead to an increased risk of certain health conditions.

Some breeds have higher predispositions to specific conditions due to their genetic makeup. For example, Persian cats may face a higher risk of Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD), Maine Coons and Ragdolls have increased incidence of Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM), and Siamese cats may be more prone to Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA). Additionally, certain physical traits selected for aesthetic reasons can sometimes impact health - such as brachycephalic (flat) faces potentially causing breathing difficulties, or taillessness affecting spinal development in some cats. Understanding these breed-specific tendencies allows breeders and owners to implement appropriate health screening and preventative care.
